                        Curative Therapies: The Economics of Game Changing Science<\/a><\/h5>\n

                        Novel, potentially curative, cancer therapies are coming to market. Recent
                        \nexperience in Hepatitis C therapy raises the question as to whether a new approach
                        \nis needed to design a pricing model that better balances resulting cost pressure
                        \nacross the delivery system. While most acknowledge systemic cost effectiveness
                        \ncurative therapies also present many challenges to payers \u2013 e.g. a spike in patient
                        \nvolume and cost as these therapies are introduced. A broad ranging dialogue
                        \nhas emerged in the cancer community that includes incorporating cost
                        \neffectiveness as part of the regulatory approval, shifting incentives for oncologists,
                        \nincreasing emphasis on early detection, integrating billing codes, bundled
                        \npayments and management fees and many others. The panel with top executives
                        \nfrom the pharma, foundation, pharmacy and provider sectors will share insights on
                        \nthis crucially important topic.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                      Winning Portfolio Strategy<\/a><\/h5>\n

                                      The many considerations involved in managing a cancer portfolio are among the
                                      \nmost important for company vitality and also play a principal role in determining
                                      \nthe breadth and rate that new therapies reach patients. Considerations include
                                      \ndrug categories (current or potential), use of approved drugs on new tumor
                                      \ntypes, product life extension, concentrating on call point strengths vs broadening
                                      \ninto new cancers, technology category strength \u2013 e.g. small molecule vs emerging
                                      \nones like CAR-T, product lifecycle status, competitive agents, possible portfolio
                                      \nswaps (e.g. NVS\/GSK) and shareholder scrutiny . Panel members from the highest
                                      \nranks of the industry — large and emerging companies — will discuss their strategic
                                      \napproach to balancing considerations they can influence and those that they can\u2019t.<\/p>\n\n\n\n

                                                                                                      \nmalignancies. Expert panelists discuss significant long-term cancer remissions,
                                                                                                      \npotential cures \u2013 in some cases \u2013 and how boosting the body\u2019s own defenses
                                                                                                      \nis producing stunning results when combined with standard anticancer therapies
                                                                                                      \nand other immunotherapies. They will also detail the experimental accines in
                                                                                                      \ndevelopment that are designed to \u201cwake up\u201d the immune system so it will trigger

                                                                                                                                                                                  Study Designs to Meet the Challenges of Personalized Cancer Medicine<\/a><\/h5>\n

                                                                                                                                                                                  Cancer biology indicates that cancer is a large number of niche diseases that may be targeted
                                                                                                                                                                                  \nwith therapies against specific molecular alterations common to multiple tumor types. This
                                                                                                                                                                                  \nmodel creates challenges for both drug development and patient care with implications for
                                                                                                                                                                                  \ninitial indication selection and design and execution of clinical trials \u2013 from first in human
                                                                                                                                                                                  \nthrough post marketing studies. Among the topics the panel will address:
                                                                                                                                                                                  \n\u2022 The availability of biomarkers to stratify trials, select patients and monitor drug response can improve
                                                                                                                                                                                  \nboth trial speed and cost, as well as enable development of companion diagnostics but at the same time
                                                                                                                                                                                  \npresent more complex validation requirements.
                                                                                                                                                                                  \n\u2022 Design of trials using surrogate end points holds great promise but validation of such endpoints is a
                                                                                                                                                                                  \nregulatory challenge. Adaptive trial designs for dose response, pK\/PD and efficacy hold promise but
                                                                                                                                                                                  \nadoption of such approaches has been slow.
                                                                                                                                                                                  \n\u2022 Cancer immunotherapies create a significant opportunity for development of combination therapies
                                                                                                                                                                                  \nbut the cost of acquiring the approved drug can significantly increase the cost of clinical trials. This may
                                                                                                                                                                                  \nnecessitate new approaches to late preclinical development as well as biomarkers that are early and
                                                                                                                                                                                  \naccurate indicators of drug response for incorporation in the clinical trial in Phase I\/II.
                                                                                                                                                                                  \n\u2022 The cost of current and next generation cancer drugs will require measuring clinical and econometric
                                                                                                                                                                                  \noutcomes in order to justify reimbursement. This may mandate larger post marketing clinical studies,
                                                                                                                                                                                  \nincluding observational trials<\/p>\n\n\n\n
