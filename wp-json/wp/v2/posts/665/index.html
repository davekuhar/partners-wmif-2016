BELMONT \u2014 Ann Romney knows a lot about political campaigns. Win or lose, there\u2019s one thing you gain for sure: a network of people with an awful lot of money.<\/p>\n

They couldn\u2019t put her husband, Mitt, in the White House, but she\u2019s now hoping everyone will get behind her to launch the Ann Romney Center for Neurologic Diseases<\/a> at Brigham and Women\u2019s Hospital. Her goal is to create a new model for research and funding, setting out to raise $50 million over the next year to help find treatments and cures for five conditions: multiple sclerosis, Alzheimer\u2019s, Lou Gehrig\u2019s (ALS), Parkinson\u2019s, and brain tumors.<\/p>\n

The Romneys are seeding the center with what Ann calls a \u201csubstantial gift.\u201d And I trust that it is big. Before Mitt\u2019s two failed presidential bids and before he served as our governor, he made a fortune as founder of Bain Capital, the Boston buyout firm.<\/p>\n

This is hardly a vanity project for Mrs. Romney. She has lived for 16 years with MS, a chronic disease of the immune system in which nerves deteriorate, causing symptoms as severe as paralysis. Her doctor, Howard Weiner, is at the Brigham and the idea for a new center came two years ago following a routine visit. Romney\u2019s MS, initially treated with steroids, has been in remission for more than a decade and is now managed by a healthy diet and exercise.<\/p>\n","protected":false},"excerpt":{"rendered":"
